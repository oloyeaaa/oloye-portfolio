I pulled 1,256 live UK MarTech job ads this month. Among mainstream marketing automation platforms, the gap between HubSpot and Marketo was larger than I expected.

| Platform | Ads mentioning it |
|---|---|
| HubSpot | 38 |
| Marketo | 4 |

That's a 9.5× difference in a dataset built from UK ads for roles like *Marketing Automation Specialist*, *Marketing Operations Manager*, and *CRM Manager*.

## Why HubSpot dominates UK demand


![Overhead view of a laptop showing data visualizations and charts on its screen.](/images/blog/hubspot-vs-marketo-uk-2026-where-to-focus-1.jpg)

*Photo by Lukas Blazek on Pexels*
Three reasons, from sitting across both worlds:

**1. SMB and mid-market hire more frequently.** HubSpot's pricing and onboarding are tuned for companies under 500 employees. The UK has a long tail of businesses in that bracket, and they all eventually need someone who "knows HubSpot."

**2. It's the default first platform.** When a marketing team's first real hire is a specialist, they're usually on HubSpot already. The vendor made buying a marketing tool feel like buying SaaS, not enterprise software.

**3. Free tier means real evaluation.** Candidates can practice on the actual platform without a license. Recruiters know this, so they ask for it.

## Why Marketo still matters — but differently

Marketo's four mentions would be bad news for a career-starter. Except Marketo isn't trying to win volume.

Marketo — and its sibling, the wider Adobe Experience Cloud — is enterprise software. The jobs are fewer, but they pay more, stay open longer, and the people who hold them tend to stay for years. Among the four Marketo ads in my scan, all were at companies over 1,000 employees. None were junior.

So the framing shifts. HubSpot is a way *in*. Marketo is a way *up*.

## What to actually do


![A person writing notes while researching online courses on a laptop in a home setting.](/images/blog/hubspot-vs-marketo-uk-2026-where-to-focus-2.jpg)

*Photo by ROMAN ODINTSOV on Pexels*
If you're starting:

1. **Spin up a free HubSpot account today.** No credit card required.
2. **Work through the HubSpot Academy certifications** — start with *Marketing Hub Software* and *Email Marketing*. They're free and still carry weight on CVs for mid-market roles.
3. **Build one real campaign.** A small business, a local cause, even a newsletter of your own. Use the CRM, the email tool, and the workflow builder. Document what you learn.

If you're two years in and looking to move up:

1. **Keep HubSpot as your base** — most mid-sized UK companies run on it.
2. **Pick up Marketo fundamentals** via Adobe's free learning path. You probably won't hit a Marketo job listing while you study, but when one lands at the right company, the competition is thinner.

## The part most trainers skip

The platform matters less than what you can prove you did with it. The UK job market will not reward you for "knowing HubSpot." It will reward you for something like:

> *Grew an email list from 0 to 4,000 with a lead magnet and a seven-email sequence, measured in the HubSpot CRM, closed out via Salesforce for attribution.*

That's the sentence. The tool is the vehicle.

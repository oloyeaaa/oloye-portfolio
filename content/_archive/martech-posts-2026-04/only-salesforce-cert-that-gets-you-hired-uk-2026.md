Earlier this month I pulled 1,256 live UK job ads mentioning Salesforce or MarTech. I wanted to answer one specific question for anyone thinking about a move into the space: **which Salesforce certification do UK recruiters actually ask for?**

Here's the ranking:

| Certification | Ads mentioning it |
|---|---|
| Salesforce Administrator (ADM-201) | 18 |
| Marketing Cloud Developer | 2 |
| Platform App Builder | 1 |

That's it. Every other Salesforce certification — Advanced Admin, Service Cloud Consultant, Experience Cloud Consultant, Platform Developer I and II, the full Marketing Cloud stack — registered **zero mentions** in my dataset.

## What this means in practice


![Two students focused on study in a contemporary library setting with laptops and books.](/images/blog/only-salesforce-cert-that-gets-you-hired-uk-2026-1.jpg)

*Photo by Mikhail Nilov on Pexels*
If you're new to Salesforce and trying to break into the UK market, the only cert that moves the needle on a CV scan is the Administrator. Everything else is a *post-hire* credential — earned once you're already in a role and trying to move up. Not a way in.

Put another way: you don't need five certs to get your first interview. You need one, plus proof you've actually built something.

## What the Admin cert actually tests

ADM-201 is the foundational exam. It covers:

- Security model (profiles, roles, permission sets, sharing rules)
- Data modelling (standard + custom objects, relationships, formula fields)
- Automation (Flow, validation rules, approval processes)
- Reports and dashboards
- User management and basic configuration

It does not cover Apex, LWC, or anything you'd call "developer" work. That's by design — it's a configuration certification, not a coding one.

## What it costs

- **Exam fee:** £155 + VAT (or $200, whichever applies).
- **Study material:** Free via Trailhead. The official Admin trailmix is enough.
- **Practice tests:** £39/month for Focus on Force or similar. Worth it — the real exam has odd phrasing that practice tests teach you to read around.
- **Time:** 6–12 weeks of evenings if you're starting cold.

Total, realistically: £200–£300 and 60–100 hours of study.

## Why the cert alone isn't enough


![Close-up of hands typing on a laptop keyboard, Python book in sight, coding in progress.](/images/blog/only-salesforce-cert-that-gets-you-hired-uk-2026-2.jpg)

*Photo by Christina Morillo on Pexels*
Here's the part most trainers gloss over. A Salesforce Admin cert is a minimum threshold, not a differentiator. The market has a lot of certified admins without hands-on experience.

What gets you to an interview, in my experience reviewing CVs:

1. **A Trailblazer profile** showing consistent activity — not just a burst of badges in exam-prep weeks.
2. **Two or three documented projects** on your own dev org. Not toy examples: a real problem, screenshots, a write-up of what you built and why.
3. **A story about stakeholders.** The top skill across all 1,256 ads was "stakeholder management" — mentioned more often than any tool. If you can't tell a story about navigating competing priorities from non-technical people, no cert will save you.

## The honest next step

If you're starting today:

1. Open a free Salesforce Developer Org this week.
2. Begin the Administrator Certification trailmix.
3. Pick a project — a small charity, a side hustle, a community group — and build them something real.
4. Write about what you built.

The cert is a milestone, not a destination. Pair it with the projects and the stories, and you're competitive. Chase a stack of certs without them, and you're invisible.

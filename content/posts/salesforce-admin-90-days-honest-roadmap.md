Most Salesforce roadmaps I've read online stop at "pass the exam and apply." The awkward bit — the gap between a cert in your hand and a first offer letter — is where most people stall.

Here's a 90-day plan that actually factors in that gap. It assumes you can put 8–10 hours a week in, no prior Salesforce experience, and you're starting today.

## Days 1–30: Foundations and Orientation


![Desk elements like calendar and pins for effective planning.](/images/blog/salesforce-admin-90-days-honest-roadmap-1.jpg)

*Photo by Towfiqu barbhuiya on Pexels*
**Goal:** Be fluent in the object model and the Trailhead ecosystem.

- Open a free Salesforce Developer Org this week.
- Work through the *Admin Beginner* and *Admin Intermediate* trailmixes.
- Create a Trailblazer.me profile and make it public.
- Spend 30 minutes a week in the UK *Salesforce User Group* community on LinkedIn. Watch who posts, what they ask about, what they share.

By the end of month one, you should be able to explain — out loud, to a non-technical person — what a Profile is, why a Sharing Rule exists, and how a standard object differs from a custom one.

## Days 31–60: Build Something Real

**Goal:** Move from modules to a live project.

This is the month most people skip, and it's the difference between passing the exam and getting hired.

- Find a real person who needs a free CRM: a local charity, a freelance friend, a community group.
- Build for them. Not a fake dataset — their actual data, their actual process, three or four custom fields that mean something to their work.
- While you build, document it. Screenshots, a Loom video, a short write-up. Publish it on LinkedIn with *exactly* what problem you solved and how.
- Start the *Prepare for Your Salesforce Administrator Credential* trailmix in parallel.

## Days 61–90: Certify and Position


![Smiling graduate proudly holding a certificate in cap and gown.](/images/blog/salesforce-admin-90-days-honest-roadmap-2.jpg)

*Photo by Ron Lach on Pexels*
**Goal:** Pass the ADM-201 exam and rework your CV around what you now have.

- Subscribe to Focus on Force practice exams (£39/month). Do two or three full timed runs before booking the real one.
- Book the exam for week 12. Having the date locked forces the preparation.
- Rework your CV *before* you pass. Lead with the project you built in month two. The cert goes in the credentials line, not at the top.
- Rewrite your LinkedIn headline. "Aspiring Salesforce Admin" signals you aren't one yet. "Salesforce Admin (Building for Charities)" signals you already do the work.

## What to do when the exam is done

Send three tailored applications a week. Not a hundred generic ones. Each should reference something specific about the company — a recent announcement, a job description detail, a name.

Keep building for whoever you built for in month two. The longer that relationship continues, the more material you have for interviews.

## Where the 90 days goes wrong

Two places, in my experience:

1. **Month two becomes another module month.** The project is uncomfortable because it involves a real human with real expectations. People avoid that by stacking more badges instead. Don't.

2. **The cert becomes the finish line.** It isn't. The cert gets the CV shortlisted. The project and the story behind it get you offered.

The plan works if you commit to the uncomfortable half.

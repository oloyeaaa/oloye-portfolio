When I moved from specialist to manager, I expected the job to be about doing more of what I was already good at, but at a bigger scale.

It's not. Enterprise marketing leadership is a completely different skill set.

## The Shift Nobody Prepares You For

As a specialist, your value is in your hands — the campaigns you build, the automations you set up, the analysis you deliver. As a leader, your value is in your decisions and the systems you create.

This is genuinely uncomfortable at first. You go from being the person who builds the thing to the person who decides what gets built, and then trusts someone else to build it.

## Three Things That Actually Matter

### 1. Stakeholder Translation

Enterprise marketing sits at the intersection of sales, product, finance, and the executive team. Every one of those groups speaks a different language and has different priorities.

Your job is translation. "Marketing generated 200 MQLs" means nothing to your CFO. "Marketing sourced pipeline worth £2.3M at a cost of £45 per opportunity" — that they understand.

### 2. System Design, Not Campaign Execution

The campaigns matter, but what matters more is the system that produces campaigns consistently. Can your team run without you for a week? A month? If not, you've built a dependency, not a team.

### 3. Strategic Patience

Enterprise deals take months. Content strategies take quarters to compound. MarTech implementations take time to show ROI. The pressure to show immediate results is real, but the leaders who create lasting impact are the ones who can hold the line on long-term strategy while delivering short-term wins.

## The Uncomfortable Truth

The transition from doing to leading means accepting that your team will sometimes do things differently than you would. And that's fine — often, they'll do it better. Your job isn't to have all the answers. It's to create the environment where the right answers emerge.

That's what enterprise leadership actually looks like. Not heroics, but systems.

## What it does

You have the idea. Six weeks later you still have a blank page. This kills that: paste the idea or a rough brief (what it is, who it is for, what it promises) and you walk away with the full outline plus the first section fully written. Real usable content, not headings with promises under them.

## How it works

It turns your brief into the product's spine: every section named with what goes in it, then it writes section one completely, in plain language, ready to keep or edit. The blank page is dead before you close the session.

## When to reach for it

"Start my product." "Outline my ebook." "Outline my course." "Kill the blank page."

## The honest bit

This is the start, not the whole build. It writes the outline and the first section; it does not write the rest, render the PDF, design the cover, or build the sales page. That full path is The Product Studio, the product-maker employee this skill is one working piece of.
